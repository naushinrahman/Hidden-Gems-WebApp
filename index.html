Toronto's Hidden Gems

Whether you're visiting Toronto for the first time or a long time Torontonian, you will definiately want to check out this list of hidden gems. From delicious rolled ice-cream to parks with a lakeside view, to a refined yet affordable ramen restaurant, this list has you covered! These spots are a must for your Summer 2023 but feel free to check them out in the fall and winter too.

Check out these local spots

Saving Mondays

A local cafe on the West End of Toronto near the intersection of Dundas West and Dupont. Saving Mondays has a selection of coffees, teas, artisan sandiwches and bagels. It's modern yet simple atmosphere is also a great place to work, read a book or just enjoy the moment. It's in the name - it's here to save you from the Monday blues.

Grey Abbey Park

If you're looking for a place to birdwatch, have a picnic, or to sit and admire the lake, this is the place for you. Grey Abbey is on a cliffside over seeing Lake Ontario. This park is located in Scarborough, away from the hustle and bustle of the city you can come here to enjoy all the beautiful birds, bunnies, and the lake.

Arctic Bites

Rolled ice cream is not just a trendy gimmick at Arctic Bites. Located in Kensington Market, this ice cream parlour offers 12 unique flavours and their delicious bubble waffles.

Guild Park

A gorgeous park on the east end of Scarborough. This picturesque park has many monuments and strucutres as well as beautiful flower arrangments in the summer. A perfect place for a photoshoot!

Midori Ramen

With locations in North York, Scarborough, and Downtown Toronto this restaurant offers authentic Japanese cuisine with great portions at an affordable price.
